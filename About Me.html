    My mom named me Shadiamond for many reasons, one of them being that she wanted me to be unique and follow my own path; stand outside of the conditions I grew up in. I have lived in Queens, NY where most people succumb to poverty and violence and others flourish beyond. Because of this, she made sure that I was well taken care of and educated in order to create a life for myself, my family, and my community members; a life that is better than the ones we were given. So, I took her hopes for me and I ran with it.I went to high school where I joined all the leadership clubs and made friends with all my peers and even some teachers. I came to Fordham and virtually did the same thing. I became a leader for freshman commuters and fully submerged myself in college life. I did all of this while dealing with economic struggle and societal impasses with things such as healthcare, racism, and climate change. Through all of these successes and failures, I have realized that the most important thing in life is being a helpful and active member of society. Whether that means me becoming an ethical journalist or a continually kind daughter, in the end, my biography must include that I made a positive difference in one way or another.

Charged

Charged is an interview based documentary that focuses on coffee as an integral part of American social culture. In Fordham specifically, students do not have to venture outside of campus to obtain caffeinated drinks because of the multitude of coffee shops on campus. Argo Tea is the most popular distributor of coffee on the Lincoln Center campus, but there are some pros and cons to this. We have observed that most students choose to have a cup of coffee before even having a proper meal, causing them to rely on sugary caffeinated beverages because of the convenience. Most professors on campus seem to share the same habits as their students. Because of this we decided to shoot the documentary from two perspectives: student and professor. Makayla is a freshman at Fordham who has expressed her need for coffee as a morning pick me up and despite Dr.Lockard also having a caffeine reliance, she gave us some larger scientific data behind why coffee should be considered, one of the more socially exempted, drugs. Through a series of interviews between the two we concluded that coffee has major social benefits and as long as it is consumed wisely, there are more benefits of coffee than other drugs like vapes and e-cigarettes.
